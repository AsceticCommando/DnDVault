## 1. Premise & Tone
- **Genre blend**
  - Multiversal conflict fantasy (gods of different universes at war)
  - Weird-high fantasy (fey-realm strangeness, broken natural laws)
  - Grimdark (Warhammer 40K × Lord of the Rings; darker than WH Fantasy)
- **Core theological conflict**
  - Forces seek to conquer / absorb all other gods & universes
  - **Central dread:** every faction offers a salvation that is *also* an ending
  - No force lets a universe simply live and change on its own terms
- **Purpose**
  - Backdrop for a D&D campaign where multiversal conflict drives the plot

---

## 2. Cosmology — "The Weft"
- **Structure**
  - Multiverse = countless **universe-curtains** hung in parallel along an axis (**the Hang**)
  - Each curtain = a complete cosmos (own gods, laws, physics woven in)
  - Distance metric: adjacent curtains are similar; far-apart curtains are strange/incompatible (→ weird-high fantasy zones)
  - Inspiration: *His Dark Materials* (universes as curtains that tear)
- **How tears happen (3 tiers)**
  - **Natural brush** — curtains sway & touch; small, rare, short-lived rips (source of "other side" legends)
  - **Disaster-scale forces** — a cosmic force billows the Hang, tearing long gashes; things blow through (campaign catastrophe engine)
  - **Cutting** — a tool/ritual opens the cloth at will (Subtle Knife–style); rare, coveted; conquerors hunt these so mortals can't evade them
- **Vertical cosmology (proposed, not locked)**
  - **The Underdrop** — the abyss/void beneath the lowest curtain (where Tehom drops severed worlds)

## 3. The Six Cosmic Forces
> Each = a pantheon + a multiversal disaster mode + a distinct real-world mythology.
> Arranged on a hexagon; opposites face across (see §4).

- ### The Evocade — *Roman*
  - Disaster: **Galewind** (wind) · Action: **tear & annex**
  - Closest to "evil"; imperial, lawful, total
  - Method: raise Galewinds → tear target curtain → pour through → **annex its physics**
  - Conquered gods become saints/organs/concepts in Evocade theology
  - Named for Roman *evocatio* (calling enemy gods to defect)
  - Pitch: annexation is the only available immortality
  - Glyph: `EVOCATIO`

- ### The Tethyne — *Greek*
  - Disaster: **Tide** · Action: **join & withdraw**
  - Most plausibly "good"; slow rhythmic swell joins cosmoses, then recedes
  - Preserves, trades, evacuates the doomed — but tides drown the weak/slow
  - Benevolent intent, indifferent mechanism
  - Glyph: `Τηθύς` (Tethys)

- ### The Sijill — *Arabic*
  - Disaster: **Fire that burns meaning, not matter** · Action: **erase & archive**
  - Burns a universe's stories/names/histories → world intact but forgotten by itself
  - Saved worlds survive only as a record in their Anthology
  - Named for Qur'anic *sijill* (scroll rolled up at the end)
  - Glyph: `سِجِّل`

- ### The Tehom — *Hebrew*
  - Disaster: **Quake** (breaks the Hang's axis) · Action: **break & drop**
  - A fault line that thinks; snaps curtains off the axis into the Underdrop
  - Earthquake-as-divine-judgment (Korah; *tehom* = primordial deep)
  - Possibly the oldest force; possibly trying to collapse everything
  - Glyph: `תְּהוֹם`

- ### The Nifling — *Norse*
  - Disaster: **Frost** · Action: **still & preserve**
  - Creeping cold slows a cosmos to total stillness (no death/growth/decay)
  - Offers permanence *without* assimilation — eternal frozen tableaux
  - From Niflheim; appeals to those most afraid of change / the Devouring
  - Glyph: `ᛁᚾᚳᛖ` (runic)

- ### The Raijin — *East Asian*
  - Disaster: **Lightning** · Action: **collide & create**
  - Arcs jump between distant curtains, fusing far-apart cosmoses for an instant
  - Births things belonging to neither; agents of monstrous novelty & prophecy
  - May not want to conquer or save — wants to see what the next strike makes
  - Glyph: `雷神`

## 4. Opposition Axes (Hexagon)
- **motion ⇅ stillness** — Evocade (tear) ↔ Nifling (freeze)
- **create ⇅ erase** — Raijin (collide into the new) ↔ Sijill (burn into one archived copy)
- **abyss ⇅ ocean** — Tehom (chaos-deep that swallows) ↔ Tethyne (world-ocean that sustains)
  - *Note:* the two water cosmologies set against each other
- **Adjacency tension**
  - Evocade & Sijill are *adjacent*, not opposite — both = "salvation by ending what you were"
  - → uneasy rivals competing for the same victims, not natural enemies

## 8. Factional Politics of the Hang
> The six are not a villain team. They are six mutually exclusive *theories of salvation*. A cosmos can only be saved one way. So the politics are about **claiming victims before a rival can** — they fight hardest at the moment of a world's death, like vultures that hate each other.

- ### The three alliances
  - **The Annexation Pact — Evocade + Sijill** (uneasy adjacents, per §4)
    - Grotesque division of labor: Evocade takes worlds worth *running* (rich physics/gods); Sijill takes worlds worth *remembering* (rich histories, dying anyway)
    - They despise each other. Sijill thinks annexation is vulgar theft; Evocade thinks archiving is cowardice (saving the memory, not the thing). Cooperate only while appetites don't overlap — which is rare.
  - **The Still Waters — Nifling + Tethyne** (alliance of convenience & contempt)
    - Both preserve rather than consume → natural partners against the Pact
    - But Tethyne preserves by *moving* (evacuation/tide); Nifling preserves by *stopping* (frost/stillness). Each sees the other's method as a slower death. Shared enemies, not shared values.
  - **The Unaligned — Tehom + Raijin** (refuse the whole frame)
    - Tehom doesn't save — it *judges & drops*. Raijin doesn't save — it *experiments*. Can't be allied with; neither wants what the other four want. Campaign wild cards.

- ### The linchpin: the TEHOM is feared by all five others
  - The only force whose "salvation" benefits *no rival*: you can't annex, archive, freeze, evacuate, or fuse a world that's been **dropped into the Underdrop**. A world the Tehom takes is lost to everyone.
  - This makes the Tehom the closest thing to a unifying threat — and the seed of the counterforce.
  - But the five can't openly unite against it without admitting their own salvations are equivalent to its judgment. So instead of a coalition: **one god breaks ranks privately.** (→ §10)

## 9. The Counterforce — The Errata
- **Origin:** treason, not consensus. Born when a single god defected rather than from any noble alliance → *compromised at its root*.
- **Name:** the **Errata** — "corrections written into the margins of a doomed cosmos."
- **Membership:** smugglers, cutters, evacuees, defector-priests from all six faiths.
- **The moral engine / catch:** the Errata is *not good*. Run by a god who still thinks in records and copies, it may not save *people* — it saves *versions* of people, and isn't always clear on the difference. Even the rescue is also an ending (ties to core dread, §1).
- **Method:** smuggles living worlds/souls across the Hang **hidden inside Tethyne evacuation convoys** (tidework as cover); likely supplemented by a few Cuttings.

## 10. The Betrayer-God — al-Kutbā / Dushara
> A two-faced figure. One being, two masks, drawn from the Apollonian/Dionysian dyad — but the duality is a *crime*, not a temperament.

- ### True identity: **al-Kutbā** ("the Writer / the Scribe")
  - A lesser **Sijill** scribe-god, once an annotator of the Anthology.
  - Began smuggling *living* worlds out of the archive instead of recording their deaths → heresy (you save the record, not the world).
  - Name is etymologically the same root as *kitab* (book) / *sijill* — his true name is woven from the cloth he betrayed. Nabataean god of writing, syncretized with Babylonian Nabu & the planet Mercury (the scribe-planet).
  - Methodologically **all archive, all order**, even now. Salvation = a perfect copy.

- ### The two masks (Greek cover-faces, both Arabic-rooted underneath)
  - **al-Kutbā → "Apollo"** — order, prophecy, the fixed record. The Sijill scribe showing through. Cold, oracular.
  - **Dushara → "Dionysus"** — Nabataean "Lord of the Mountain," vine & bull; ecstasy, dissolution, living flux. The warm rescuer refugees actually follow.

- ### The crime: he wears his twin brother
  - al-Kutbā has no Dionysian *side* — he **wears his dead-or-captive twin Dushara as a mask.** His first "correction," his proof of concept, was his own brother.
  - Refugees won't follow a filing cabinet, so the scribe performs the vine-god: borrowed warmth over a scribe's intentions. Every soul drawn in by "Dionysus" is processed by "Apollo."

- ### Dushara's fate: **ANTAGONIST** (locked)
  - Dushara was **imperfectly archived** — al-Kutbā's copy is *diverging from the original*. The Dionysian dissolution-god is **waking inside his own mask.**
  - He claws back toward flux, ecstasy, and the right to *change*, while al-Kutbā fights to keep the record fixed. The counterforce now has a god at war with the mask he wears.
  - A being archived alive by his own brother, Dushara wants the Hang to **burn into change** rather than be kept as copies. Natural drift toward the **Raijin** (collide & create) — and, at his darkest, sympathy with the **Tehom's** collapse: better oblivion than to be a kept edition again.
  - **Consequence — two endgame bosses in one body:** the party can end up fighting al-Kutbā (who would keep them as perfect copies forever) OR Dushara (who would dissolve them, and everything, into living chaos), OR be caught between the brothers' war for control of the shared body. No version is "safe."

- ### Recommended reveal structure (three turns of the screw)
  1. PCs believe in **Dionysus** — their warm rescuer.
  2. Mid-campaign: he's a mask worn by **Apollo/al-Kutbā** — their salvation was *archival, not living*. (The false-rescue pivot.)
  3. Late game: **Dushara is stirring** inside the mask — an antagonist neither fully brother nor fully copy.

- ### Loose thread (pre-loaded)
  - al-Kutbā's mythological consort is **al-ʿUzzā** ("the Most Powerful," Venus/evening star). Available as a Sijill power who *didn't* defect and now hunts him — a relationship already in the source material.

## 11. The Party as Seam-World (PC origin)
- PCs come from **different homeworlds** → the party itself is a tiny stitched seam-world.
- They were each smuggled across the Hang **inside Tethyne convoys** — i.e. collected by the Errata as survivors / samples / "corrections."
- Early on they'll believe the **Tethyne** (or "Dionysus") saved them — benevolent rescuers.
- The reveal: their salvation was a hidden **Sijill heretic running a copy-operation** under Tethyne cover. They were never refugees. They were *annotations*.
- They may not yet know they're a deliberate collection. → strong Act One reveal.

## 12. Adventure Structure — Classical Greek Tragedy
> The campaign is built on the anatomy of Attic tragedy. The fall is caused not by villainy but by a virtue (the scribe's love, expressed as the will to preserve) pushed past its limit. **Closest model: Euripides' *The Bacchae*** — al-Kutbā is Pentheus (the king who cages Dionysus) and Dushara is Dionysus (who destroys the man who denied him), fused in one body.

- ### Tragic anatomy mapped to canon
  - **Hamartia** (the fatal mistaken act): al-Kutbā archiving his own brother to "save" him.
  - **Hubris**: the scribe's belief that a perfect copy is salvation.
  - **Peripeteia** (reversal): the rescue revealed as imprisonment.
  - **Anagnorisis** (recognition): the party learning *what they are* (copies) and *what their savior did* (fratricide-by-archive).

- ### The movements (each = one or more hooks/sessions)
  1. **PROLOGOS — the world before the wound.** Played straight, no irony. Each PC, in their own dying homeworld, is saved by the warm rescuer "Dionysus" (Galewind tearing the sky / Quake-fault opening). *Make the players love the rescue before it turns.*
  2. **PARODOS — the chorus enters.** Party meets aboard the Tethyne tide-convoy & becomes a unit. The refugees = the chorus (the soon-devoured). First dread: the convoy is *hunted* — al-ʿUzzā reads the tide-trails. Dread as rumor.
  3. **EPISODES & STASIMA — rising action (alternating crisis & reflection).**
     - *1st episode — leakage:* a rescue curdles; the Reveler's warmth slips for a heartbeat. Deniable.
     - *1st stasimon:* quiet session, refugees & PCs reckon with what they half-saw. Dread, not combat.
     - *2nd episode — the Annotation:* party finds a homeworld in the Anthology, subtly *wrong*. Hamartia surfaces as evidence.
     - *2nd stasimon:* the **Tehom** drumbeat grows under everything — the fault that thinks, drawing closer.
  4. **PERIPETEIA — the reversal (structural hinge, now spans TWO sessions).** The reveal is a slow poisoning of trust, not a single beat. **Pace: 1–2 clues per session; 3 clues + alignment triggers the confrontation.** Six findable clues exist; any three pointing the same way is enough.
     - *Session A (clues 1–2):* the first two clues are **damning** — they indicate the rescuer behaved badly (double-counted manifest, the refugee who remembers wrong). Strong suggestive evidence, no proof. Trust starts to rot. End on an unresolved **decision** (confront now, or wait for proof?) left to ride across the real-world break.
     - **THE BREAK (interstitial trap, run *between* the two sessions):** if the party comes back resolved to accuse on the two damning clues alone, the confrontation happens at the *top of Session B, before they have the exonerating clue*. al-Kutbā then produces the exonerating Clue 3 himself — calm, wounded, true. The party has accused a grieving rescuer to his face; they carry a small hamartia inside the big one. Consequence (wired to §13): Tethyne ledger **−1** (or **−2**/Enmity if public/hostile); Errata = **story** consequence (al-Kutbā turns wary, withholds, accelerates — the rest of Session B runs with a guarded patron). The doubled guilt — right about the crime, wrong about the man — is the tragedy working. The trap punishes *certainty*, not investigation, rhyming with al-Kutbā's own sin.
     - *Session B (clue 3 + confrontation):* if they waited, the third clue is **exonerating** — genuine grief, not malice (the obituary with wet ink). Only with all three does the *tragic* picture resolve: a loving act that was also a crime. Then the feast confrontation (three beats: Accusation → Mask Flickers → The Choice).
     - Reveal payload unchanged: they are **editions**; "Dionysus" is **al-Kutbā**; the face is a **dead/waking twin**.
  5. **ANAGNORISIS — the recognition.** Doubled: the party recognizes (a) their own nature as copies, and (b) that "Dionysus" was a murdered twin, *and the twin is waking.* The **Errata civil war** ignites here — the counterforce fractures along the brothers' fault line (some follow order/al-Kutbā, some warmth/Dushara).
  6. **CATASTROPHE — *The Bacchae* enacted.** Dushara ruptures: the caged Dionysian destroys the order that denied him — tearing for a new body (the archived PCs), and/or dragging the Hang toward the **Raijin's** chaos or the **Tehom's** collapse. The two-bosses-one-body endgame detonates. The fall is the hamartia's debt called in.
  7. **EXODOS — what survives.** Which editions remain? Did anyone become *real* — uncopied, free to change & die on their own terms? Core dread's final statement: every salvation was an ending; the only true rescue was the freedom to be lost.

- ### The tragedy-vs-agency needle (design note)
  - Classical tragedy is **deterministic** (the fall is foreordained; pleasure = dramatic irony). D&D is **player-driven** (agency is the point). These pull against each other.
  - **Resolution:** the *fall* is inevitable but its *meaning* is the players' to author. They can't save everyone (tragedy holds), but whether the catastrophe is horror, liberation, or mercy is theirs to decide (agency holds). Keep this needle in mind for every movement.

## 13. The Ledger of the Six — Faction Reputation System
> Full GM reference in **THE_WEFT_Ledger.docx**. Summary below.

- **Two dials per faction:**
  - **Standing (the Account):** a ledger of *favors owed*, integer −3…+3. Positive = they owe you (Credit); negative = you owe them (Debt); 0 = even. Moves in whole favors via fiction, never grinding. Below −3 → **Enmity** (faction actively works against you).
  - **Power (Ascendancy):** GM-side dial 1–5, how strong the force is across the Hang. Independent of the party; driven by the war (§8).
- **Boons need BOTH Credit and Power.** Tier gates: *Passage* (Credit ≥1, Power ≥1) · *Gift* (Credit ≥1, Power ≥2) · *Rescue* (Credit ≥2, Power ≥3).
- **Symmetric skeleton, asymmetric flavor:** every faction offers the same three tiers at the same cost — only the flavor & catch differ, expressing its method of ending. **Every rescue has a catch** (salvation = ending).
  - Evocade: passage via tears / annexing blade / rescue makes you *theirs*.
  - Sijill: unwritten-step / copy-book / rescue saves a *copy* (rhymes with §11 editions).
  - Tethyne: tide-passage / merge-pearl / rescue withdraws you *plus something unchosen*.
  - Raijin: collision-step / spark-forge / rescue *changes* you.
  - Tehom: the Long Drop / plumb-of-judgment / rescue drops you somewhere *lost*. **Its debts are the only un-buyable ones — mechanically why all fear it (§8).**
  - Nifling: frozen-path / stasis-locket / rescue *freezes* you (time passes without you).
- **Debt has teeth (player choice locked):** the party CAN fall into Debt. Factions **call it in on their own schedule**, expressed in their method (Evocade annexes something you love; Sijill edits your memory; Tehom drops something forever). You may **refuse** a call-in, but refusal converts Debt → **Enmity**. Always offer the choice; make both options cost.
- **Cross-faction spillover is LOOSE (player choice locked):** helping one faction moves a rival's Account only when the act directly targets that rival; lean on §8 alliances/axes, not a fixed matrix.
- **The Errata / "Dionysus" is NOT a faction account** — it's a story thread (§10–§12). Post-mask-reveal, a Sijill-adjacent debt may attach.
