# Caves of Chaos
*Source: Heroes of the Borderlands, p. 2* 

Beyond the Keep on the Borderlands hide the Caves of Chaos, a network of monster-infested caves where by a wicked cult lurks. Danger awaits foolhardy adventurers who enter the caves—but so do treasure and glory.

## DM Secret

This is for the Dungeon Master's eyes only. Unless text appears in a read-aloud box (like the one below), don't read it to the players.

## Getting Started

The Caves of Chaos Map displays all the locations characters can visit in the Caves of Chaos. The player version of this map doesn't reveal the caves' contents or names, only the cave mouths and their respective elements. Share that map with your players.

To begin, read the following boxed text aloud:

> [!readaloud] 
> 
> You pass through a dense forest of gnarled trees with grasping roots, the woods twisting as if to ward you off. Undeterred, you emerge from the tangled thicket into a stark ravine. Its rocky walls rise steeply one hundred feet or so above. Several dark cave mouths pockmark the walls at varying heights. The air is stale and quiet.
> 
> Among the rocks and dead wood that litter the ravine's floor are the bones of animals and explorers, picked clean by scavengers. Vultures leer at you hungrily from barren branches.
> 
> Where do you want to go?

### Cave Descriptions

Each description of a cave has these sections:

- **Getting Started.** This section provides instructions for the DM and introduces the cave with thematic read-aloud text.  
- **Features.** Important features about the cave, such as its lighting, appear here.  
- **Locations.** This section details the cave's areas and its contents, including any monsters.  
- **DM's Map of the Cave.** This map displays the locations of monsters, traps, and treasure within the cave.  

### Rhythm of Play

When the characters enter an area for the first time, do the following:

- **Step 1.** Describe the Area. Describe what the characters perceive in the area. Focus on what they see, hear, or smell.  
- **Step 2.** Ask the Players, "What Do You Do?" Players tell you what their characters do or say.  
- **Step 3.** Narrate the Results. The player characters reap the consequences of their actions as outlined in the text. This might involve one or more [D20 Tests](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/d20-test-xphb.md) (see the D&D Beyond Basic Rules (https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/dnd/br-2024)) to determine the outcome. It could also lead to combat (see below).  
- **Step 4.** Repeat. When the player characters reach another area of the cave, repeat this procedure.

### Resting

Threats abound within the caves. The characters can take only [Short Rests](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/short-rest-xphb.md) within them. To take a [Long Rest](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/long-rest-xphb.md), they must first exit any cave they're in. For more on "resting", see the D&D Beyond Basic Rules (https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/dnd/br-2024).

### Leaving the Caves

The characters can leave the Caves of Chaos any time they choose, provided they aren't inside a cave or engaged in combat. If they return to a cave later, it is as they left it.

### Character Advancement

In this adventure, the characters can gain levels by completing caves. To complete a cave, the characters must explore every area within it.

The characters gain levels by achieving the following milestones:

- **Completing Two Caves.** After the characters fully explore two caves, they advance to level 2.  
- **Completing Three More Caves.** After the characters fully explore a total of five caves, they advance to level 3.  

Each of these level advancements occurs only once. If the characters are already level 3, they don't gain levels by exploring the caves.

## A: Kobold Lair

A band of kobolds recently stole a dragon egg and brought it to these caves to eat. But when the egg hatched and a screeching dragon wyrmling emerged, the kobolds quickly realized they bit off more than they could chew.

The kobolds tried to appease the fussy wyrmling with treasure, but they can't agree on what to do next. Should they return the wyrmling to its nest, or should they raise it themselves?

> [!note] 
> 
> **Summary.** The beginning of each cave presents its central conflict, as well as optional ways to increase or decrease the difficulty. If the characters are level 1, don't increase the difficulty.
> 
> **Bold Terms.** When a monster's name appears in **Bold** type, that's a visual cue to say, "Hey, DM, you should get this stat block ready. You're going to need it."
^01d

You can add monsters, such as [Giant Fire Beetles](3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/beast/giant-fire-beetle-xmm.md), to the cave to make this scenario longer and more difficult, or you can reduce the number of [Kobold Warriors](3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/dragon/kobold-warrior-xmm.md) to make it easier and shorter.

##### Pit Trap

This 10-foot-deep pit is covered by a large, dirty cloth. When a character steps on the cloth, it gives way, dumping the character into the pit, at which point the pit is no longer hidden. A creature that falls into the pit takes `dice:1d6|noform|noparens|avg` (`1d6`) Bludgeoning damage.

Rules for detecting and escaping the trap are as follows:

- **Detecting the Trap.** The pit is poorly camouflaged. A character who examines the trapped area easily discovers the pit. Once detected, the pit can be safely avoided by sidling along its perimeter.  
- **Escaping the Pit.** The pit's walls have cracks big enough to serve as handholds, allowing trapped creatures to climb out easily.  

A character falling into the pit alerts the kobold in area A2, who approaches the pit and crows, "Got 'em!" The encounter in that area then occurs here.

> [!note] 
> 
> **Rhythm of Play.** Here's an example of how the pit trap in area A1 might play out:
> 
> - **Describe the Scene**. Read the boxed text aloud.  
> - **Ask the Players, "What Do You Do?"** You might ask how the player characters enter the cave. If they charge brashly ahead, the character in the lead falls into the pit. However, if a player says their character looks carefully, they spot the trap.  
> - **Narrate the Results**. Once the pit's presence becomes known, describe the pit and the narrow ledge around it.  
> 
> Once the players know the pit is there, ask them how their characters bypass it. If they come up with a creative solution not described in the text, err on the side of leniency and fun. Whenever you think a rule might be necessary, see the D&D Beyond Basic Rules (https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/dnd/br-2024).
> 
> Repeat this process for the other areas in this cave, modifying it based on their contents.

As an [Influence](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/actions.md#Influence) action, a character can try to appease the kobold and make one of the following ability checks. Consider these examples:

**DC 10 Charisma ([Deception](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Deception)).** The character tricks the kobold with an outlandish lie.

**DC 10 Charisma ([Performance](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Performance)).** The character amuses the kobold with a jaunty tune.

> [!note] 
> 
> **Ability Checks.** When the outcome of an action is uncertain, the game uses a `dice:d20|noform|noparens|avg` (`d20`) roll to determine success or failure. This is called a [D20 Test](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/d20-test-xphb.md). An ability check is one type of D20 Test. Here's how to run the ability check in area A2:
> 
> - **Describe the Scene**. Paint a mental picture of the kobold either by describing its words and actions or by actively roleplaying it—giving it a voice like an actor in a role.  
> - **Ask the Players, "What Do You Do?"** If the players aren't sure what to do, suggest ways they could respond to the kobold, like trying to deceive or persuade it.  
> - **Narrate the Results**. The Difficulty Class (DC) for each listed ability check is 10. If the total of the `dice:d20|noform|noparens|avg` (`d20`) and its modifiers equals or exceeds 10, the check succeeds. On a 9 or lower, it fails. In either case, the area's description tells you what happens.  
> 
> - **Example.** A Rogue tries to deceive the kobold with a Charisma ([Deception](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Deception)) check. The player rolls a `dice:d20|noform|noparens|avg` (`d20`) and adds their character's [Deception](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Deception) modifier (+4) for a total of 14. The check succeeds—the kobold believes the lie!

##### Soothing the Wyrmling

The wyrmling knows the kobolds aren't its parents, but it doesn't know where its nest is. The wyrmling doesn't yet have a name, which only compounds its misery.

As an [Influence](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/actions.md#Influence) action, a character can try to calm the wyrmling and make one of the following ability checks. Consider these examples:

**DC 10 Charisma ([Deception](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Deception)).** The character mollifies the wyrmling with a promise they don't intend to keep.

**DC 10 Charisma ([Persuasion](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Persuasion)).** The character assures the wyrmling they're here to help.

**DC 10 Charisma ([Performance](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Performance)).** The character soothes the wyrmling with a gentle lullaby.

A character who offers the wyrmling at least 10 GP has Advantage on this check. On a successful check, the wyrmling stops wailing, and its attitude improves to [Friendly](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/friendly-attitude-xphb.md), as do the attitudes of all kobolds in the cave. The wyrmling then asks the characters whether it should stay with the kobolds or return home. The kobolds honor the wyrmling's choice, which corresponds with the characters' recommendation.

> [!note] 
> 
> **Advantage/Disadvantage.** Sometimes a [D20 Test](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/d20-test-xphb.md) is modified by special situations called [Advantage](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/advantage-xphb.md) and [Disadvantage](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/disadvantage-xphb.md), which reflect positive and negative circumstances, respectively. See the D&D Beyond Basic Rules (https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/dnd/br-2024) for more on Advantage and Disadvantage.
> 
> In area A5, if a character gives the dragon wyrmling at least 10 GP, that character has Advantage on an ability check to influence it.
> 
> - **Roll Two D20s.** When a roll has either Advantage or Disadvantage, roll a second `dice:d20|noform|noparens|avg` (`d20`) when you make the roll. Use the higher of the two rolls if you have Advantage, and use the lower roll if you have Disadvantage.  
> - **Example.** A Cleric gives the wyrmling 10 GP and then hums a tune to soothe it. The Cleric takes the [Influence](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/actions.md#Influence) action and makes a Charisma ([Performance](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Performance)) check with Advantage. The player rolls two d20s: 15 and 6. The player uses the higher roll and adds the Cleric's [Performance](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Performance) modifier (+2) for a total of 17, which exceeds the DC of 10, and the check succeeds. If the check had been made with Disadvantage instead, the total of 8 would've failed.  
^041

On a failed check, or if the characters try to steal from its meager hoard, the wyrmling fights. The cowardly kobolds abstain from battle. If the wyrmling defeats all the characters, they wake up 1 hour later outside the cave with 1 Hit Point each.

> [!note] 
> 
> **Saving Throws.** The wyrmling's Acid Breath action (see the wyrmling's stat block) prompts another type of [D20 Test](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/d20-test-xphb.md): a saving throw. Saving throws—also called saves—are named for the ability modifiers they use; in this case, characters use Dexterity to dodge an exhalation of acid. If a character is targeted by this effect, the player rolls a `dice:d20|noform|noparens|avg` (`d20`) and adds the character's Dexterity save modifier. A save's result is detailed in the effect that caused it.
^043

## B: Mephit Lair

Sturdy fortifications and clean water make this abandoned subterranean outpost an appealing den for thieves and monsters. Before the prior inhabitants left, they stored some enticing treasure here, guarded by a monstrous shape-shifter called a mimic. Since then, a trio of impish elementals and kuo-toa fanatics have made the cave their home.

You can add monsters, such as [Giant Centipedes](3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/beast/giant-centipede-xmm.md), to the cave to make this scenario longer and more difficult, or you can remove the [Kuo-Toa Marauders](3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/aberration/kuo-toa-marauder-hotb.md) or one of the [Steam Mephits](3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/elemental/steam-mephit-xmm.md) to make it easier and shorter.

To begin, read the following boxed text aloud:

**DC 10 Charisma ([Performance](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Performance)).** The character amuses the ogre with a small performance, such as a dance or magic trick.

On a failed check, or if a character tries to undercut Oggdug's price, the ogre starts combat, grumbling that the characters are "puny cheapskates." If the ogre drops to half his Hit Points or fewer, he calls to Cindertooth in area E3 for aid.

##### Hiring the Ogre

Oggdug is accustomed to other denizens of the Caves of Chaos hiring him for mercenary work. His rate is 50 GP per job, but first-time customers get a 50 percent discount (25 GP). Cindertooth gets half of Oggdug's earnings.

If the party pays the ogre his asking price, he joins them for a single fight in any of the caves except for the two caves he says are off-limits (cave G and cave K). After the fight, the ogre returns to this cave unless the characters pay him for another bout. The second time the characters hire the ogre and every time afterward, his rate is 50 GP per job.

**"Oggdug Sad."** If a character asks Oggdug about either of the off-limits caves, the ogre sighs mournfully and tells them his tragic story. Oggdug once shared this cave with his older brother, Zurf, before Zurf was killed by an owlbear near its den (cave G). Cultists later animated Zurf's half-eaten corpse, which now guards the cultists' lair (cave K). Oggdug now shuns both caves.

##### Treasure

The filthy bearskins conceal a pile of equine bones, a wheel of cheese, a keg of brandy, and a lumpy sack containing 60 GP. The ogre warns anyone who goes near the bearskins that he'll "give 'em a good stomping" if they continue snooping. He makes good on his threat.

## J: Gnoll Lair

Evil gnolls—tall, shaggy fiends with hyenoid heads—have turned this abandoned shrine into their lair. The gnolls heard rumors that treasure is hidden somewhere in this cave, but they've been unable to find it due to an illusion placed by a long-dead mage. Vicious and hot-tempered, the gnolls have little patience for those who interrupt their search.

You can add monsters to the cave to make this scenario longer and more difficult. One or more [Bugbear Warriors](3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/fey/bugbear-warrior-xmm.md) might have formed a tenuous alliance with the gnolls. Conversely, you can reduce the number of [Gnoll Warriors](3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/fiend/gnoll-warrior-xmm.md) to make the scenario easier and shorter.

### Gnoll Lair Features

- **Lighting.** The cave is unlit, and its denizens rely on [Darkvision](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/senses.md#Darkvision) to see. Area descriptions assume the characters have a light source or other means of seeing in [Darkness](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/darkness-xphb.md).

- **Avoiding the Trap.** A Medium or smaller creature can slip through the gap between the doors with a successful DC 13 Dexterity ([Acrobatics](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Acrobatics)) check; failing this check triggers the trap.

- **Triggering the Trap.** If the door is moved, the scraps clatter to the ground. The noise alerts all the gnolls in the cave, who cackle as they rush to dispatch intruders.

## K: Shrine of Evil Chaos

The Cult of Chaos performs evil rites in this cave, subtly drawing monsters to inhabit the Caves of Chaos and bring strife to the Borderlands. The current cult leader is Ivlis, the keep's former priest. Her cruel assistant, Narthus, is always here, and other cultists dwell close by (see cave G). Undead guardians defend the shrine and the Chaos Bell—the source of the cult's foul magic—at the shrine's heart.

You can add monsters, such as [Cultists](3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/humanoid/cultist-xmm.md), to the cave to make this scenario longer and more difficult, or you can reduce the number of [Skeletons](3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/undead/skeleton-xmm.md) or [Zombies](3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/undead/zombie-xmm.md) to make the scenario easier and shorter.

- **Lighting.** Unless otherwise noted, [Bright Light](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/bright-light-xphb.md) from red-flamed wall sconces fills the cave's areas.
