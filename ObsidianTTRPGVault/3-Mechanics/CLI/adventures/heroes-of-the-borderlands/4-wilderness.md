# Wilderness
*Source: Heroes of the Borderlands, p. 2* 

The wilds of the Borderlands teem with dangerous animals and ravenous monsters.

## DM Secret

This is for the Dungeon Master's eyes only. Unless text appears in a read-aloud box (like the one below), don't read it to the players.

![](file://adventure/HotB/107-03-001.adventure-cover.webp#center)

## Getting Started

The Wilderness Map displays all the regions characters can visit in this section of the adventure. The player version of this map doesn't reveal the contents of each wilderness region, only the general geographic features. Share that map with your players. The Caves of Chaos and the Keep on the Borderlands aren't labeled on this version of the map so the players don't confuse them with wilderness regions.

To begin, read the following boxed text aloud:

> [!readaloud] 
> 
> Your adventuring party enters the wilderness. Mysteries await you in this untamed land. Where do you want to go?
^1b9

The Wilderness Regions table summarizes the regions in the order they appear within this section. If fully explored, each region provides about 60–90 minutes of gameplay.

Each region is roughly the same difficulty, but the Trail region functions as a tutorial with helpful sidebars. If this is your first time as the DM, encourage the players to start there. Otherwise, have them choose a region shown on the map.

Once the player characters choose a region, go to that region and review its "What You Need to Play" section.

**Wilderness Regions**

| Region | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| 1: Trail | Friendly travelers and no-good bandits alike use this dirt road. |
| 2: Woods | These majestic forests shelter wily fey and occult secrets. |
| 3: Fens | Territorial lizardfolk and a spy for hire lurk in this wetland. |
| 4: Tamarack Stand | Giant spiders plague this vast stand of conifers. |

### Resting

The dangers of the wilds keep travelers on their toes. The characters can take only [Short Rests](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/short-rest-xphb.md) while exploring a wilderness region. To take a [Long Rest](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/long-rest-xphb.md), characters must visit the keep (see "Keep on the Borderlands"). For more on "resting", see the D&D Beyond Basic Rules (https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/dnd/br-2024).

### Leaving the Wilderness

The characters can leave the wilderness any time they choose, provided they aren't in combat. If they return to a region later, it's as they left it.

### Character Advancement

In this adventure, the characters can gain a level by exploring different regions. After the characters complete at least one encounter in all four regions, everyone in the party gains 1 level. This advancement occurs only once. If the characters are already level 3, they don't gain a level for achieving this milestone.

#### Looted Wagon

> [!readaloud] 
> 
> A wagon stands on the trail ahead. The driver and horses are nowhere to be seen.
^1ce

The wagon was looted by bandits, who stole the horses and scared away the driver. Led by a local menace named Pral, these bandits frequently accost travelers along the trail (as happens in the "Bandit Ambush" encounter described later in this adventure).

A character who inspects the wagon can make one of the following ability checks to learn more about it. Consider these examples:

**DC 13 Intelligence ([Investigation](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Investigation)).** The character deduces the wagon was ambushed by bandits.

**DC 13 Wisdom ([Survival](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Survival)).** The character discovers a striking bootprint left by Pral. The heel is monogrammed with the letter *P*.

**DC 13 Wisdom ([Perception](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Perception)).** The character finds treasure the bandits missed (see below).

> [!note] 
> 
> **Ability Checks.** When the outcome of an action is uncertain, the game uses a `dice:d20|noform|noparens|avg` (`d20`) roll to determine success or failure. This is called a [D20 Test](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/d20-test-xphb.md). An ability check is one type of D20 Test. Here's how to run the ability check in the "Looted Wagon" encounter:
> 
> - **Describe the Scene**. Read the boxed text to paint a mental picture of the scene.  
> - **Ask the Players, "What Do You Do?"** If the players aren't sure how to respond, suggest ways they could glean more information, like looking for tracks.  
> - **Narrate the Results**. The Difficulty Class (DC) for each listed ability check is 13. This number represents the difficulty of the task. If the total of the `dice:d20|noform|noparens|avg` (`d20`) and its modifiers equals or exceeds 13, the check succeeds. On a 12 or lower, it fails. The encounter's description tells you what happens on a successful check. In this encounter, nothing happens on a failed check, but the character can try again.  
> 
> - **Example.** The Cleric searches the wagon and makes a Wisdom ([Perception](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Perception)) check. The player rolls a `dice:d20|noform|noparens|avg` (`d20`) and adds their character's [Perception](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Perception) modifier (+5) for a total of 17. The check succeeds, and the Cleric finds the treasure.  
^1cf

#### Game Trail

> [!readaloud] 
> 
> You spot a regal elk on the trail beside a dense thicket. Startled by your approach, the elk lopes into the thorny thicket. "Blast!" cries a husky voice from somewhere in the brambles.
^1db

A character can safely navigate the thicket to the source of the noise by succeeding on one of the following ability checks. Consider these examples:

**DC 12 Wisdom ([Survival](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Survival)).** The character follows the narrow game trail used by the elk.

**DC 12 Intelligence ([Nature](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Nature)).** The character knows how to avoid the brambles' thorns.

On a failed check, a character scrambles through but takes `dice:1d4|noform|noparens|avg` (`1d4`) Piercing damage from the thorns.

##### Hidden Glen

A hidden glen lies on the other side of the game trail. Dozens of elk graze here in peace. Hiding in the bushes is Haldryck, a [Friendly](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/friendly-attitude-xphb.md) innkeeper who likes to hunt. (Haldryck also appears in "Keep on the Borderlands".)

Haldryck asks the characters to keep his hunting spot a secret. If they agree, he gives the characters 5 GP and encourages them to spend it at his inn when next they visit the keep.

> [!note] 
> 
> **Social Interaction.** Player characters meet many different people while adventuring. As DM, you assume the roles of these nonplayer characters, whom you can embody through either active or descriptive roleplaying (detailed below). Players also use one or both of these approaches to roleplay their characters.
> 
> - **Active Roleplaying.** In active roleplaying, you speak with a character's voice and might echo their body language, like an actor taking on a role. You still need to describe things that can't reasonably be acted out.  
> 
> An active approach to roleplaying Haldryck in the "Game Trail" encounter might involve you pretending to hide behind imaginary foliage and speaking in hushed tones.
> 
> - **Descriptive Roleplaying.** In descriptive roleplaying, you draw on your mental image of the character, saying what the character does and how they do it.  
> 
> In the "Bandit Ambush" encounter, a descriptive approach to roleplaying Pral, a smarmy bandit captain, might involve you describing his flashy smile or confident swagger. You might tell the players that he holds out his hand expectantly for the characters' gold.

#### Bandit Ambush

> [!readaloud] 
> 
> A handsome man in a green-and-gold tunic and fancy boots saunters out of the bushes. "Hello," he says casually as he blocks your path. "I'm Pral. Perhaps you've heard of me. Who am I kidding? Of course you have!"
> 
> Pral draws a sword. "Your money and your equipment, please. This is a robbery."
^1e1

![](file://adventure/HotB/113-03-004.pral-c.webp#center)

[Pral](3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/humanoid/pral-hotb.md) leads a local gang of bandits. Three [Bandits](3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/humanoid/bandit-xmm.md) hide in the nearby bushes. Pral and the bandits are [Hostile](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/hostile-attitude-xphb.md) to the characters.

If the characters don't relinquish their gold or talk their way out of the situation, Pral gives the signal for the bandits to spring out (see the "Fighting the Bandits" section).

##### Adjusting the Difficulty

To make the encounter easier, you can reduce the number of [Bandits](3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/humanoid/bandit-xmm.md), or you can add another bandit to increase the difficulty.

##### Avoiding a Fight

As an [Influence](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/actions.md#Influence) action, a character can try to defuse the situation and make one of the following ability checks. Consider these examples:

**DC 13 Charisma ([Deception](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Deception)).** The character lies to Pral to gain safe passage, perhaps by pretending to be a fellow bandit.

**DC 13 Charisma ([Persuasion](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Persuasion)).** The character convinces Pral that violence isn't necessary.

**DC 13 Charisma ([Intimidation](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Intimidation)).** The character pressures Pral into reconsidering the ambush.

> [!note] 
> 
> **Advantage/Disadvantage.** Sometimes a [D20 Test](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/d20-test-xphb.md) is modified by special situations called [Advantage](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/advantage-xphb.md) and [Disadvantage](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/disadvantage-xphb.md), which reflect positive and negative circumstances, respectively. See the D&D Beyond Basic Rules (https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/dnd/br-2024) for more on Advantage and Disadvantage.
> 
> In the "Bandit Ambush" encounter, Pral is [Hostile](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/hostile-attitude-xphb.md), so characters have Disadvantage on checks to influence him and thus avoid a fight.
> 
> - **Roll Two D20s.** When a roll has either Advantage or Disadvantage, roll a second `dice:d20|noform|noparens|avg` (`d20`) when you make the roll. Use the higher of the two rolls if you have Advantage, and use the lower roll if you have Disadvantage.  
> - **Example.** A Rogue tries to smooth things over with Pral by pretending to be a bandit. The Rogue makes a Charisma ([Deception](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Deception)) check with Disadvantage.  
> 
> The player rolls two d20s and adds their character's [Deception](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Deception) modifier (+4) to both results to get two totals: 16 and 9. They use the lower roll, which falls short of the DC of 13, so the check fails—Pral and his bandits start combat! If this check had instead been made with Advantage, it would have succeeded.
^1e4

On a successful check, Pral lets the characters pass unharmed. On a failed check, Pral signals the bandits to emerge (see the following section).

#### Fighting the Bandits

In the case of combat, read the following to begin:

> [!readaloud] 
> 
> Pral *tsks* in disapproval. He snaps his fingers, prompting more bandits to spring from the bushes with weapons drawn. In an instant, you're surrounded—roll Initiative!
^1e7

Pral and his bandits are brigands, not murderers. A character reduced to 0 Hit Points by Pral or his cohorts has the [Unconscious](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/conditions.md#Unconscious) condition but is [Stable](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/stable-xphb.md). If all the characters are defeated by Pral's band, they wake up 1 hour later on the trail with 1 Hit Point—and 10 fewer Gold Pieces or no Gold Pieces if they have fewer than 10 GP—each, along with a mocking note penned by Pral. At your discretion, Pral might reappear to the characters the next time they explore this region to rob them again.

Four [Cultists](3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/humanoid/cultist-xmm.md) conduct a ritual around this ancient statue. They belong to the Cult of Chaos, a faction that delights in death and disarray. They are [Hostile](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/hostile-attitude-xphb.md) toward those not garbed in their cult's attire but don't immediately notice the characters.

The cultists continue intoning until their ritual is complete or interrupted. If disturbed, they fight.

##### Adjusting the Difficulty

To make this encounter easier, reduce the number of [Cultists](3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/humanoid/cultist-xmm.md). Conversely, you can make this encounter more challenging by adding one or more [Zombies](3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/undead/zombie-xmm.md), which aid the cultists as undead thralls.

##### Fooling the Cultists

The cultists are [Indifferent](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/indifferent-attitude-xphb.md) toward any character wearing a Cultist's Robe, which they might have obtained in the Caves of Chaos (see "Caves of Chaos"). If a character approaches in this disguise, the cultists solemnly motion for the character to join the ritual, but they begin combat if the character refuses.

##### Completing the Ritual

If the characters join the ritual—or observe it without drawing attention—the statue's runes begin to glow with an eerie red light. After 10 minutes, the sky darkens, and the statue suddenly splits in two with a loud crack. Moments later, a wicked, disembodied laugh echoes throughout the forest. The cultists then disband.

##### Treasure

The characters can take robes from the defeated cultists. For each robe they take, give the players a [Cultist's Robe](3-Mechanics/CLI/items/cultists-robe-hotb.md).

This sacred spring is infused with primal magic.

##### Healing Water

A character who takes an action to drink from the spring regains 5 Hit Points. Once a character regains Hit Points from the spring, they can't do so again until they finish a Long Rest.

##### Destroying the Chaos Bell

If the party has the Chaos Bell from the Shrine of Evil Chaos (see "Caves of Chaos"), a character can submerge the evil artifact in this sacred spring. If this is done at night while the spring is bathed in moonlight, the bell turns to glittering ash.

If a character approaches the mound or disturbs the treasure (see below), the lizardfolk leap out and start combat. Characters who failed to detect the lizardfolk before the ambush have [Disadvantage](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/disadvantage-xphb.md) on their Initiative rolls. The lizardfolk fight fiercely in defense of their possessions.

#### Spy's Campsite

> [!readaloud]

This campsite belongs to [Jacko](3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/npc/jacko-hotb.md), a notorious spy for hire. Jacko hides in the surrounding foliage. He is [Indifferent](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/indifferent-attitude-xphb.md) toward the characters.

##### Detecting Jacko

As a [Search](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/actions.md#Search) action, a character can survey the campsite for clues to its inhabitant's whereabouts and make one of the following ability checks. Consider these examples:

**DC 15 Wisdom ([Perception](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Perception)).** The character spots the glint of Jacko's blade in the reeds.

**DC 15 Wisdom ([Survival](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Survival)).** The character spots faint tracks that lead to the spy.

On a successful check, Jacko emerges from the tall reeds and commends the party on finding his hideout. He then offers his services to the party.

![](file://adventure/HotB/126-03-010.jacko-c.webp#center)

##### Hiring Jacko

Plucky do-gooders aren't Jacko's usual clientele, but for 25 GP, he offers to join the characters in a single fight anywhere in the wilderness. If the party contains a Rogue or a character with the Criminal background, Jacko offers a 5 GP discount. The shady halfling advertises his services as "all inclusive" and "no questions asked" while performing a cutthroat gesture.

If a player character hires Jacko, whenever the characters roll Initiative in the wilderness, ask that player if they'd like Jacko to assist them in the fight. If so, Jacko joins the fray alongside the party. After the fight, Jacko returns to this location unless the characters hire him again.

#### Sword Ferns

> [!readaloud] 
> 
> As you trudge through the mud, you tumble into a ravine of razor-sharp ferns. To continue, you must navigate this treacherous gully.
^215

To forge a path through the ferns, each character must succeed on one of the following checks:

**DC 15 Strength ([Athletics](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Athletics)).** The character hacks a path through the ferns with a weapon or stick.

**DC 15 Wisdom ([Survival](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Survival)).** The character finds a safe trail through the ferns used by wild animals.

On a failed check, the character takes `dice:1d6|noform|noparens|avg` (`1d6`) Slashing damage from the ferns on their way through the gully.

![](file://adventure/HotB/128-03-009.giant-spider-c.webp#center)

##### Giant Webs

The giant spiders have woven a 20-foot [Cube](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/cube-area-of-effect-xphb.md) of thick, sticky webs between the trees to snare prey. The webs are [Difficult Terrain](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/difficult-terrain-xphb.md), and the area within them is [Lightly Obscured](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/lightly-obscured-xphb.md).

The first time a creature enters the webs on a turn or starts its turn there, it must succeed on a DC 12 Dexterity saving throw or have the [Restrained](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/conditions.md#Restrained) condition until it breaks free or the webs are destroyed.

The webs are flammable and laden with dry pollen and old animal carcasses. If the webs take Fire damage, the entire 20-foot Cube of webs ignites, and each creature Restrained by the webs takes `dice:2d4|noform|noparens|avg` (`2d4`) Fire damage. The webs then burn away and are destroyed at the end of the turn.

##### Treasure

#### Pine Seeds

> [!readaloud]

| dice: 1d8 | Encounter |
|-----------|-----------|
| 1 | A [Goblin Warrior](3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/fey/goblin-warrior-xmm.md) suspended in a massive web calls for help. If freed, the [Friendly](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/friendly-attitude-xphb.md) goblin helps the characters defeat two [Hostile](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/hostile-attitude-xphb.md) [Giant Spiders](3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/beast/giant-spider-xmm.md) that arrive moments later. |
| 2 | The characters come across a swath of trees rotted by a strange blight. A character who examines the trees can make a DC 15 Intelligence ([Arcana](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Arcana)) check; a successful check reveals the affliction stems from evil magic wrought by occult rituals. |
| 3 | Four [Friendly](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/friendly-attitude-xphb.md) [Commoners](3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/humanoid/commoner-xmm.md) around a campsite invite the characters to camp with them for the evening; the characters can take a [Long Rest](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/long-rest-xphb.md) at this campsite. |
| 4 | Two [Bugbear Warriors](3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/fey/bugbear-warrior-xmm.md) use a rusty log saw to fell a mighty oak. The [Hostile](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/hostile-attitude-xphb.md) lumberjacks fight any who disturb their work. |
| 5 | A [Hostile](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/hostile-attitude-xphb.md) and ravenous [Owlbear](3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/monstrosity/owlbear-xmm.md) searches for prey. It fights the characters on sight. |
| 6 | Oleira (see appendix B) the trader is picking mushrooms. She can point the characters to Xanthe's hut (see the "Hermit's Hut" encounter). |
| 7 | A majestic stag appears in a picturesque clearing. If the characters approach the stag peacefully, it leads them to the sacred spring (see the "Sacred Spring" encounter). Otherwise, it flees. |
| 8 | The characters are caught in a lightning storm. Each character must succeed on a DC 15 Dexterity saving throw or take `dice:2d8\|noform\|noparens\|avg` (`2d8`) Lightning damage. |
