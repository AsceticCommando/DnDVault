# Keep on the Borderlands
*Source: Heroes of the Borderlands, p. 2* 

Rising from rugged crags amid a harsh countryside, the Keep on the Borderlands is a bastion of peace amid an otherwise brutal wilderness. The keep's stalwart defenders protect their community from the forces of chaos that would revel in its fall.

## DM Secret

This is for the Dungeon Master's eyes only. Unless text appears in a read-aloud box (like the one below), don't read it to the players.

![](file://adventure/HotB/067-02-001.adventure-cover.webp#center)

## Getting Started

The Keep on the Borderlands Map displays all the locations characters can visit in the keep. Share this map with your players.

To begin, read the following boxed text aloud:

> [!readaloud] 
> 
> You ascend a winding road through a forested and mountainous country. Ahead, atop a sheer-walled stone plateau, looms a great and stoic keep. Armed sentries along the outer wall peer down at you, ready to welcome new heroes or repel would-be invaders.
> 
> Two armed guards stand at the front gate. They hail you and motion for you to approach.
^10f

The player characters start just outside the keep. The first time they arrive at the keep, the characters must proceed to the outer gatehouse (detailed later in this chapter), which functions as a tutorial with helpful sidebars. You can skip this location for subsequent visits to the keep unless the player characters want to return to it.

After the characters visit the gatehouse or whenever they return to the keep, have the players choose a location shown on the map. The Keep Locations table summarizes noteworthy sites within the keep in the order they appear. Each location provides approximately 30–60 minutes of gameplay, depending on the nature of the characters' visit.

Once the player characters choose a location, go to that location and review its "What You Need to Play" section.

## Exploring the Keep

The player characters can visit any location in the keep, meeting and interacting with the citizens who reside there. Each keep location employs one or more NPCs, typically representing the most important person at a location, such as an establishment's owner or highest ranking representative.

The map of the keep isn't to scale, but you can use it to track the characters' current whereabouts. If this is your first time as Dungeon Master, ask your players to keep the party together; it's easier if the player characters visit each location as a group.

### Location Descriptions

Each description of a keep location has the following sections:

- **Features.** This section details noteworthy features about the location.  
- **NPCs.** Each keep location contains one or more NPCs, each reflected by a digital NPC Card nested within the section.  
- **Services.** Services offered at the location, such as goods for sale or rooms for rent, appear here. If a player character pays for a good or service, collect Gold Pieces equal to its cost from that player.  
- **Quest.** This section contains a quest unique to the location. Most take place in the keep, though some encourage the characters to venture beyond its walls. Each quest can be completed only once.  

### Social Interaction

The keep offers ample opportunities for characters to socialize with its inhabitants. All NPCs in the keep are initially [Indifferent](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/indifferent-attitude-xphb.md) toward the characters. For more about [NPC attitudes](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/attitude-xphb.md), see the D&D Beyond Basic Rules (https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/dnd/br-2024).

### Combat

![](file://adventure/HotB/069-02-017.guard-c.webp#center)

Combat is prohibited in the keep. If you need game statistics for an NPC, reference the recommended stat block listed on their NPC Card.

At your discretion, characters who antagonize peaceful members of the keep might attract the attention of the soldiers of the keep, of whom there are many (use the [Guard](3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/humanoid/guard-xmm.md) stat block). Those who refuse to keep the peace might be arrested and detained for a few days, fined up to 50 GP, or ousted from the keep until they can behave.

### Resting

Unlike in the other two adventure settings, the keep is a safe place to rest and resupply. The characters can freely take [Long Rests](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/long-rest-xphb.md) in the keep. For more on "resting", see the D&D Beyond Basic Rules (https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/dnd/br-2024).

### Leaving the Keep

The player characters can leave the Keep on the Borderlands whenever they choose. As they accumulate treasure and advance in level, the party might return to spend their hard-earned wealth, rest, or follow up on previously undertaken quests.

### Character Advancement

Though there are quests to complete in the Keep on the Borderlands, the characters don't gain levels in this section. Each quest offers its own reward, usually in the form of gold or favors.

### Gatehouse Features

The gatehouse has the following features:

- **Drawbridge.** The keep's first line of defense is a steep ditch, 10 feet wide and 10 feet deep, in front of the fortress. The only sure means to cross the dry moat is the drawbridge at this gatehouse.  
- **Gate.** The stone arch behind the drawbridge contains an iron portcullis and large oak doors.  
- **Towers.** Stairs inside each square, 30-foot-tall tower connect the ground floor to the roof. Arrow slits in the tower walls and roof battlements help archers defend the keep.  

> [!note] 
> 
> **Features.** Some features about a location help you set the mood or describe aspects of the location, while others detail important gameplay information, like the depth of the keep's moat (in case a creature falls into it).
^122

![Banner for the Keep](file://adventure/HotB/072-02-002.banner.webp#center)

### Gatehouse Services

The gatehouse offers the following services:

- **Lower the Drawbridge.** The drawbridge usually remains raised night and day. To lower or raise the bridge, wheels in both towers must be turned to release or tighten the drawbridge's chains. The portcullis operates in a similar manner.  

> [!note] 
> 
> **Services.** Each location offers unique services. Shops typically offer goods for sale, which might be associated with a handout, while other locations offer more abstract benefits. An NPC at the location can summarize its services to the characters.
^125

- **Orientation.** The gate guards, Bartho and Gala, can describe and give directions to any location in the keep. They can also tailor recommendations to a traveler's needs. For example, if a character wants to purchase weapons or armor, the guards point them to the trader's shop (area 6).

Once the drawbridge is lowered, Bartho and Gala introduce themselves and encourage the characters to lend their services to the keep.

Instead, players simply say where they want their characters to go. None of the locations within the keep has or requires its own map; as DM, you help the players visualize these places through your descriptions.

Read the following boxed text aloud:

Before they venture into the Borderlands, the characters can report to the fortress (area 14) for more information on how they can further the interests of the castellan and his advisers.

> [!note]

#### Bartho's Reward

The guards here have orders to help out around the keep between shifts, but Bartho claims he must rest instead to stay sharp when he's on duty. He pulls the characters aside and asks them to complete quests around the keep for a small reward.

When the characters return to the outer gatehouse after completing at least three quests around the keep, Bartho thanks them and gives each character 5 GP.

> [!note] 
> 
> **Treasure.** Rewards within the keep usually come in the form of Gold Pieces (GP). Whenever a player character finds gold, give that player Gold Pieces equal to the listed value, which is tracked on their character sheet. Encourage players to split their treasure as evenly as possible.

## 2: Barn

This barn shelters the keep's livestock. It also serves merchants and travelers who need a place to stable their mounts while visiting the keep. Animals housed at the barn receive a box stall or pen, food, and grooming.

When the characters arrive at the barn, read the following boxed text aloud:

> [!readaloud]

### Barn Features

The barn has the following features:

- **Box Stalls.** The barn has roomy stalls for up to six Large mounts.  
- **Livestock Pens.** Cows, chickens, pigs, and other barn animals occupy several livestock pens.  
- **Loft.** Golden piles of hay are stored in a loft above the animal enclosures.  
- **Trough.** Stabled animals drink water from a common trough along the stalls.  

### Barn Services

The barn offers the following services:

- **Chores.** Once per day, the characters can assist Cornflower with chores around the barn, such as collecting freshly laid eggs, feeding and grooming animals, and milking cows and goats. In exchange for their help, Cornflower pays them 1 GP each.  
- **Dairy Products.** For 1 GP, Cornflower sells a pail's worth of milk or a slab of freshly churned goat butter (customer's choice).  
- **Hit the Hay.** Cornflower sympathizes with travelers who can't afford to stay at the inn (area 8). She lets them sleep in the loft for free.  

### Quest: Billy Goat Wranglin'

After introducing herself and explaining the barn's services, Cornflower confesses she could use the characters' help. Read the following text aloud:

> [!readaloud] 
> 
> "Last night, I accidentally left the goat pen unlocked—and this mornin', all the goats were gone! I'm sure they're still within the keep, but I need to tend to the other animals here and make sure no others get loose. Would you track down the missin' goats and return them to the barn?"
^136

Cornflower offers the characters 1 GP for every goat they bring back. The number of missing goats equals the number of player characters.

#### Finding the Goats

The goats are scattered throughout the keep. Roll `dice:1d12|noform|noparens|avg` (`1d12`) for each missing goat, rerolling duplicates. Keep these results secret. Each result corresponds to a numbered location on the Keep on the Borderlands Map where a goat can be found. For example, on a 6, a goat is outside the trader's shop (area 6). To find the goat, the characters must go to that location, though they don't have to venture inside.

A character can narrow their search by making one of the following ability checks. Consider these examples:

**DC 10 Wisdom ([Survival](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Survival)).** The character follows hoofprints in the dirt.

**DC 10 Charisma ([Persuasion](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Persuasion)).** The character asks passersby if they've seen a loose goat.

On a successful check, the character learns the location of one goat whose whereabouts the party doesn't already know.

#### Returning the Goats

When the characters arrive at a goat's location, they find it grazing outside the structure and can coax it back to the barn. When the characters return a goat to Cornflower, she pays them 1 GP as thanks.

### Apartments Features

The apartments have the following features:

- **Apartments.** Each two-story apartment is a section of the same large tenement building. The apartments have identical amenities: a stove, a small drawing room, a bedroom, and a storage area.  
- **Rain Barrel.** A large barrel near the building holds clean water for washing dishes and clothes.  

### Apartments Services

The apartments offer the following services:

- **Buy Gems.** Lenk can buy gems the characters find in their adventures. Lenk exchanges gems at this location for their value in gold. All the gems in this adventure are worth 100 GP; this rate includes Lenk's appraisal fee.  
- **Rent an Apartment.** Characters can rent an apartment for 100 GP per month.  

### Quest: Breaking the Bank

After detailing the services he offers, Lenk tells the characters he's exploring a new line of business. Read the following boxed text aloud:

> [!readaloud] 
> 
> "With reports of bandits along the trail and the occasional thief here in the keep, I plan to sell strongboxes to help citizens and travelers secure their valuables. I've received a few samples made by local crafters who hope to partner with me in this new venture, but I won't stake my reputation on shoddy handiwork.
> 
> "I need to determine which of the strongboxes are fit for sale, and you look like a capable lot. Care to test your mettle on some metal?"
^13f

If the characters agree, Lenk hands each of them a locked iron strongbox and instructs them to try to pilfer its contents however they please. Each strongbox contains a modest amount of gold. If a character can get into a strongbox, they can keep its contents, though Lenk doesn't reveal what's inside.

#### Cracking the Strongboxes

A character can make one attempt to open a strongbox through any of the following means:

- **Breaking the Strongbox.** Each strongbox is a Tiny object with AC 19, 5 HP, Immunity to Poison and Psychic damage, and Vulnerability to Cold damage. A character who reduces a strongbox to 0 Hit Points with a single attack or spell destroys the strongbox but not its contents.  

**Picking the Lock (A character who has Thieves' Tools**). A character that can use Thieves' Tools to pick the strongbox's lock, doing so with a successful DC 15 Dexterity ([Sleight of Hand](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Sleight%20of%20Hand)) check.

- **Prying Open the Lid.** A character can force open a strongbox's lid with a successful DC 15 Strength ([Athletics](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Athletics)) check.  

At the DM's discretion, other methods of opening a strongbox might exist.

#### Reward

Inside each strongbox is 5 GP. If the characters manage to penetrate all the strongboxes, Lenk grouses about the boxes' poor craftsmanship. If all the strongboxes thwart their attempts, Lenk pays each character 1 GP as a consolation and says there are nobler skills to master than thievery.

### Smithy Services

The smithy offers the following service:

- **Leather Branding.** For 1 GP, Kalista can brand anything made of leather, such as Leather Armor or a Spellbook, with the keep's coat of arms.  

Kalista makes armaments but doesn't sell them. She forges them exclusively for the keep's armed forces.

### Quest: Tools of Destruction

If a character asks to purchase armor or weapons, Kalista explains why she no longer sells those to outsiders. Read the following boxed text aloud:

> [!readaloud] 
> 
> "I used to sell arms and armor to would-be heroes like you. I had heard reports of foolhardy adventurers falling victim to brigands and monsters. I shrugged those stories off for a time.
> 
> "Last winter, the castellan's forces thwarted an assault on the keep. After the onslaught, I was sickened to learn that many of the attackers' weapons bore my maker's mark. By selling to outsiders, I had unwittingly armed our enemies. I vowed then never to forge another weapon for anyone but the keep's soldiers. I haven't since."
^148

The keep's soldiers occasionally bring confiscated weapons to Kalista for destruction.

#### Weapon Smelting

Kalista recently received a cartload of battered and rusted weapons seized by the keep's defenders. She asks the characters to help melt them down.

The disarmament process takes 1 hour. To aid Kalista with it, the characters must succeed on the following ability checks in this order:

**DC 13 Strength ([Athletics](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Athletics)).** The character piles the weapons in the forge and undertakes the tough labor needed to properly melt them down.

**DC 13 Dexterity ([Sleight of Hand](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Sleight%20of%20Hand)).** The character carefully tips a crucible of molten metal into an ingot mold without spillage.

If at least one character succeeds on each check, the party completes the disarmament. The characters can retry failed checks, but if they accumulate three failures, Kalista dismisses them with a sigh, insisting she can handle the rest of the job herself.

If the characters successfully help Kalista destroy the weapons, she pays them each 5 GP.

### Quest: Fire Ghost

If the characters don't bring up the unusual sign on the storeroom door, Ruckus soon mentions it and asks for their help in resolving the matter. Read the following boxed text aloud:

> [!readaloud] 
> 
> "I've been smelling smoke in the storeroom for the past few days. Yesterday I peeked inside and saw a cloaked figure smoldering like an ember—surely it was a fire ghost! I threw a flagon of ale at it to put out the fire, but I haven't dared to enter the room since. Someone braver than me should lay the ghost to rest. Maybe you could do it?"
^150

#### The "Ghost"

The only thing haunting the shopkeeper is his own reckless disorganization. Ruckus recently relocated some inventory to a shelf in his storeroom; the merchandise included a hefty magnifying glass. On sunny days, the light through a high window focuses through the lens on a dusty old cloak, causing it to smolder. It's a major fire hazard.

Ruckus's decision to leave the storeroom alone compounds the danger. If the problem isn't dealt with soon, a conflagration is inevitable.

#### Investigating the Scene

Characters can get at the truth by investigating the jumbled storeroom, where they discover a singed cloak stained with dried ale.

##### Gathering Clues

Characters in the storeroom can gather clues by succeeding on one of the following ability checks. Consider these examples:

**DC 13 Intelligence ([Nature](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Nature)).** At noon each day, sunlight from the window shines on the cloak.

**DC 12 Wisdom ([Perception](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Perception)).** High on a shelf near the storeroom window, a magnifying glass protrudes from an open box.

![Storeroom Box](file://adventure/HotB/080-02-006.storeroom-box.webp#center)

#### Solving the Case

After inspecting the storeroom, the characters can draw their conclusions. A character who considers the evidence and succeeds on a DC 13 Intelligence ([Investigation](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Investigation)) check correctly deduces the source of the smoke and the risk it poses to Ruckus's shop. A character who gathered one or both clues has [Advantage](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/advantage-xphb.md) on this check.

If the characters explain the situation, Ruckus is relieved to hear his shop isn't haunted and corrects the fire hazard. As thanks, he pays each character 10 GP and encourages them to spend it here.

### Quest: Chug Champion

The tavern's signature game is a nightly contest called chug champion.

#### Playing the Game

To play chug champion, participants—called "chuggers"—line up at the bar and pay 5 GP each to receive three pints of either heady ale or non-alcoholic cider (their choice). The goal is to drink the pints as quickly as possible over 3 rounds.

Each round, each participating character must succeed on a DC 12 Constitution saving throw to quaff the pint in one go. On a failed save, the character drinks too slowly and is eliminated along with a handful of other chuggers. Otherwise, they proceed to the next round. A character who succeeds on all three saves wins the game.

If multiple characters succeed on all three saving throws, compare the results of those chuggers' last saves, rerolling any ties. The character with the highest result places first, the character with the second-highest result places second, and any other characters place third.

![A Tavern Stein](file://adventure/HotB/091-02-010.stein.webp#center)

#### Winning the Game

The winner receives 25 GP. Second- and third-place chuggers win 15 GP and 10 GP, respectively.

### DM Secret

[Ivlis](3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/npc/ivlis-hotb.md) secretly leads the Cult of Chaos, an evil cult that feeds on destruction and disarray. Her supposed religious journey is a cover for her occult activities, which are detailed in the "Caves of Chaos".

### Temple Features

The box is unlocked. ### Quest: Priestly Duties

In Ivlis's absence, Fazzir is having trouble keeping up with the flood of new responsibilities. He asks the characters to help him with two clerical tasks.

![A Holy Symbol](file://adventure/HotB/096-02-012.holy-symbol.webp#center)

#### Impromptu Blessing

A married couple asked Fazzir to give them a blessing for safe travel for a romantic getaway. Fazzir needs two assistants to perform the ceremony.

To conduct the ceremony properly, two characters must each take the [Study](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/actions.md#Study) action and make a DC 10 Intelligence ([Religion](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Religion)) check. On a successful check, the couple departs the temple with cheer. On a failed check, the couple postpones their trip, uneasy about what their journey might bring.

#### Occult Symbol

Last night, a vandal defaced the temple with a strange symbol. Fazzir doesn't recognize the symbol. He asks the party to scrub away the graffiti.

A blood-red bell is splattered onto the side of the temple in runny paint. A character who inspects the symbol and succeeds on a DC 13 Intelligence ([Arcana](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Arcana)) check recognizes the bell as the mark of the Cult of Chaos and an omen of future turmoil. If informed about the symbol's meaning, Fazzir finds the revelation deeply troubling. The mark washes off easily, but its stain on the priest's mind lingers.

#### Reward

After these tasks, Fazzir gives each character 3 GP regardless of their performance. He is just grateful for their support.

> [!note] Good-Faith Discounts
> 
> It's unlikely that the characters will accumulate the gold required to revive a party member. At the DM's discretion, Fazzir might waive any of the fees on the Sacred Services Handout for those he believes show heroic potential.
^good-faith-discounts

### Quest: Testing the Defenses

Andrella has an unusual request. Once she learns the characters are adventurers, she dismisses the guards and says the following to the party:

> [!readaloud] 
> 
> "I want to observe how my soldiers would fare in an invasion scenario. As adventurers, you might be able to simulate such a threat.
> 
> "I'd like you to sneak past the guards stationed here after sunset and steal a pennant from the other side of the gate. As part of this exercise, you can't harm any on-duty soldiers, but I'll explain the situation if any of you are detained. "Are you up for the challenge?"
^197

In exchange for their work, Andrella offers the characters a total of 25 GP.

#### Infiltrating the Guardhouse

During the night, eight soldiers stand watch outside the gatehouse: six on the roof and another two on the ground before the open gate. If necessary, use the [Guard](3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/humanoid/guard-xmm.md) stat block to represent each soldier.

As an action, a character can try to bypass the soldiers and make one of these ability checks:

**DC 15 Dexterity ([Stealth](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Stealth)).** The character slips past the soldiers undetected.

**DC 15 Strength ([Athletics](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Athletics)).** The character scales the keep's sheer wall between regular patrols.

**DC 15 Charisma ([Deception](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Deception)).** The character fools the soldiers with a disguise or convincing lie.

On a successful check, the character bypasses the soldiers undetected. On a failed check, two soldiers spot the character and take them into custody, but other characters can continue the mission.

### Debrief with Andrella

If at least one character reaches the pennant without getting caught, Andrella meets them afterward. She pays the party 25 GP as promised. If no characters were spotted, Andrella throws in an extra 15 GP and chastises the soldiers they evaded.

If all the characters are detained before anyone in the party can grab the pennant, Andrella visits the characters in a sparsely furnished detention cell and thanks them for testing her soldiers. She informs the soldiers about the training exercise and smugly pays the characters 25 GP on their release.

### Inner Bailey Features

The inner bailey has the following features:

- **Cavalry Stable.** The bailey contains a stable with thirty horses. These war-trained horses serve as mounts for the keep's cavalry.  
- **Guard Towers.** Four towers rise above the bailey, one in each corner. The smallest, to the southeast, holds up to eight guards. The southwest and northeast towers each hold one dozen guards, as well as food stores and other supplies. The Great Tower to the northwest is 60 feet tall.  
- **Watches.** Eight soldiers, two atop each tower, watch over the inner bailey at all hours. Use the [Guard](3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/humanoid/guard-xmm.md) stat block for these soldiers if necessary.  

### Inner Bailey Services

The inner bailey offers the following service:

- **Weapon Upgrade.** The keep ordered too many crossbows, which untrained recruits can't wield effectively. Shannik wants to trade the weapons for simpler ones. In exchange for 25 GP and a Shortbow, he can provide a character with a [Hand Crossbow](3-Mechanics/CLI/items/hand-crossbow-xphb.md).  

### Quest: Shannik's Chores

Shannik hires the characters to complete a couple chores around the inner bailey. The characters can refuse his request, but Shannik asks with increasing insistence each time they visit the inner bailey.

#### Disrupting the Charge

Shannik wants to train two green recruits for the cavalry. He has them ride around the bailey for 10 minutes, spearing the hanging rings with lances.

During the exercise, Shannik asks the characters to disrupt the training by distracting the horses or their riders without causing injury to either. A character can do so by succeeding on one of the following ability checks. Consider these examples:

**DC 13 Wisdom ([Animal Handling](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Animal%20Handling)).** The character guides a horse off course.

**DC 13 Charisma ([Intimidation](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Intimidation)).** The character tries to startle a horse or its rider.

![A Cavalry Lance Laden with Training Rings](file://adventure/HotB/100-02-014.lance.webp#center)

#### Catapult Installation

Shannik wants to install a catapult atop the Great Tower to defend against invaders. To do so, the characters must succeed on the following ability checks in this order:

**DC 15 Strength ([Athletics](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Athletics)).** The character hauls supplies out of the stores in the southwest tower, across the bailey, and up the Great Tower.

**DC 13 Intelligence ([Investigation](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Investigation)).** The character correctly assembles the catapult according to its blueprints.

**DC 13 Wisdom ([Perception](3-Mechanics/CLI/rules/skills.md#Perception)).** The character calibrates the catapult by test-firing it at a target outside the keep's walls.

This task takes 1 hour. If at least one character succeeds on each check, the party completes the task. The characters can retry failed checks, but if they accumulate three failures, Shannik dismisses them, grumbling about their incompetence.

#### Reward

In exchange for completing a chore, Shannik pays a character 5 GP. A character can complete each of Shannik's chores only once. Thereafter, the sergeant greets the characters with a respectful nod whenever they visit this area.

## 14: Fortress

The castellan governs the keep from its fortress, a stone stronghold solidly built to withstand assault. Multiple towers rise above the main building, which is 30 feet tall. The fortress shelters emergency resources, municipal chambers, and the keep's armed forces. Militaristic banners and upholstered furniture decorate its dignified interior.

When the characters arrive at the keep fortress, read the following boxed text aloud:

> [!readaloud] 
> 
> Two round towers with pointed roofs flank this tiered stronghold. Archers march along its stone parapets, watching for signs of trouble. Two guards stand outside the fortress's iron doors; they acknowledge passersby with stern nods.
^1a4

### Fortress Features

The fortress has the following features:

- **Armory.** Barding and lances for the keep's cavalry—thirty troops strong—are stored here, though the equipment is off-limits to the characters.  
- **Barracks.** Sparsely furnished quarters throughout the fortress can collectively accommodate up to thirty-six soldiers at once.  
- **Great Hall.** A spacious hall in the fortress hosts ceremonies, proclamations, and other receptions.  
- **Private Chambers.** Two lavishly decorated rooms are reserved for guests of the keep, while another belongs to the castellan.  
- **Towers.** Two peaked towers rise from the fortress.  
- **Undercroft.** Beneath the fortress are cellars containing vast provision stores, a cistern, and a dungeon with four stout cells for detainees and prisoners. If Yvette was arrested at the bank (see area 7), she is detained in one of the cells.  

### Fortress Services

The fortress offers the following services:

- **Defense.** In times of crisis, the castellan can muster up to thirty-six trained soldiers from the fortress to defend the keep. The fortress also provides refuge during incursions.  
- **Seat of Government.** During the day, the castellan and his two assistants conduct business in the main building, where they hold audience with citizens and visitors of the keep.

#### Winvarle the Castellan

The keep's noble castellan, an human man named Winvarle, holds court daily in the great hall. A keen strategist, Winvarle casts his vigilant gaze toward the Caves of Chaos, where rumors of a sinister cult have surfaced (see the "Caves of Chaos").

When the characters visit Winvarle, he issues the following quest:

> [!readaloud] 
> 
> "Your arrival is fortuitous. I've heard whispers of a nefarious cult—the self-proclaimed Cult of Chaos—that reportedly operates in a cave network to the northeast. I believe the source of the cult's power resides in one of those caves.
> 
> "These lands yearn for heroes. For the good of the Borderlands and the people under my charge, I humbly ask you to investigate these rumors and, if need be, quash this evil before it reaches the keep."
^1b0

Winvarle claims this quest is its own reward, but he adds that the Caves of Chaos abound with treasure, hoping to entice any characters who seek only to fill their coffers.

Castellan Winvarle also encourages the characters to meet with his adviser and scribe, who might have other tasks for them to complete.

### Ending the Adventure

Castellan Winvarle's celebration marks the end of this adventure, but there's still plenty to explore after that. The characters can continue to adventure in the Keep on the Borderlands, the Caves of Chaos, and the Wilderness (detailed in each respective section) until they've experienced all the Borderlands has to offer. You can also use them to tell stories of your own.

If you wish to advance these characters beyond level 3 and create new adventures for them, you need the core rules: the "Player's Handbook", the "Dungeon Master's Guide", and the "Monster Manual".
