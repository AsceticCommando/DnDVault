A **two-session investigation module** (D&D 5e, ~6–8 hours). The reversal the whole
campaign bends toward: the party has been saved and sheltered by a warm, vine-crowned
god they know as "Dionysus." Across two sessions they gather evidence that the rescue
was something else, then force the mask off.

By the end they should know three things they cannot un-know:
they are not refugees but **editions** (archived copies); "Dionysus" is a mask worn by
**[[2-World/People/al-Kutbā.md|al-Kutbā]]**, a Sijill scribe-god who saves the record,
not the world; and the face he wears belonged to his twin
**[[2-World/People/Dushara.md|Dushara]]**, who is beginning to wake.

## The clue spine (not equal)
- **Clue 1 — The Manifest That Counts Twice** (DAMNING) ·
  [[2-World/Places/The Tally-Car.md|the tally-car]]
- **Clue 2 — The Refugee Who Remembers Wrong** (DAMNING) ·
  [[2-World/People/Oresh.md|Oresh]]
- **Clue 3 — The Obituary With Wet Ink** (EXONERATING) ·
  [[2-World/Places/The Vintner's Car.md|the Vintner's Car]] reliquary
- Optional 4–6: the huntress's warning ([[2-World/People/al-ʿUzzā.md|al-ʿUzzā]]),
  the vines that aren't growing, the slip.

Clues 1 & 2 make the rescuer look like a predator; Clue 3 reveals genuine grief.
Only all three resolve the *tragic* truth: **a loving act that was also a crime.**

## The Break (interstitial trap)
If the party accuses him on the two damning clues alone, the confrontation happens
before they have Clue 3 — al-Kutbā produces the exonerating evidence himself. They
carry a small hamartia inside the big one. Consequence (Ledger of the Six): Tethyne
**−1** (or **−2**/Enmity if public/hostile).

## Confrontation (the feast, three beats)
Accusation → the Mask Flickers (Dushara surfaces) → the Choice (stay and be kept, or
go as a fragile original). **Do not resolve it cleanly.**

*Continuity: this is movement 4 of the campaign's tragic structure and seeds
movement 5 (Anagnorisis) and the Errata civil war.*
